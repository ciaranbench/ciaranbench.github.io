Currently I am a neuroengineering postdoc at the UCL Ear Institute using deep learning to improve the efficacy of hearing aids.

Previously (2022) I undertook a short postdoc at the University of Exeter in the School of Physics and Astronomy where I used deep learning to aid in the analysis of biomedical Infrared/THz hyperspectral images.

From 2018-2022, I was a PhD student at University College London where I investigated learned approaches to solving non-linear inverse problems in photoacoustic imaging - in particular, the problem of quantifying haemoglobin concentrations from reconstructed image data (quantitative photoacoustic tomography).

Concurrently (Sep-Dec 2019), I undertook a placement as a consultant data scientist at MediWise where I constructed ML models to facilitate non-invasive measurements of blood glucose concentrations.

I also dabble in algorithmic sports betting and have deployed an AI-driven web application for UFC fight prediction (developed the full stack). Aside from this, I occasionally compete in competitive e-sports events (Super Smash Bros. Ultimate).
